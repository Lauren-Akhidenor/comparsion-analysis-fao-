## Project Overview
This project presents a **comparative analysis of inflation, producer price changes, and agricultural production trends** (2019â€“2025) across selected countries using **FAOSTAT data**.  

The focus is on **potatoes and wheat**, evaluating how macroeconomic pressures translate into producer returns and consumer costs across **developed and developing economies**, including Nigeria, Egypt, Jordan, Kenya, Ireland, the Netherlands, the United Kingdom, and the United States.  

---

## Data Source
- **Source:** FAOSTAT (UN Food and Agriculture Organization)  
- **Dataset:** Food Prices and Agricultural Producer Statistics  
- **Period:** 2019â€“2025  

**Key Variables:**
- **Inflation Rate (YoY %):** Consumer-level inflation  
- **Cumulative Inflation Since 2018 (%):** Long-term inflationary trend  
- **Producer Price Change (YoY %):** Annual change in farm-gate prices  
- **Producer Production ($):** Agricultural output value (Potatoes and Wheat)

## Rationale for Crop Selection
- **Potatoes and Wheat** represent staple crops in developed and developing economies  
- Illustrate **disparities in price transmission** between consumer and producer levels  
- Highlight smallholder **vulnerability to global inflation and market shocks**  
- Serve as a lens to inform **policy interventions and development strategies**  

---

## Key Insights
- Inflation affects consumers and producers differently; gains are uneven across economies  
- Developed nations (USA, UK) maintain **high efficiency and lower volatility** in potato production  
- Developing countries (Nigeria, Kenya) face **larger inflationary shocks** relative to output growth  
- The **gap between consumer and producer prices** emphasises economic imbalance in agricultural value chains
